    What are Game Position Presets?

    Game position presets are shared screen offsets (X/Y pixel shifts) that align the crosshair with a specific game's aim point. Only presets matching YOUR resolution and display mode are shown, because game aim points are pixel-specific per setup.

    Tips for In-Game Use

    • All hotkeys work even inside fullscreen games (with borderless windowed mode).
    • Start with big nudges (Ctrl+Alt+Shift+Arrows) to get close, then fine-tune with small ones.
    • Turn on debug grid (Alt+Shift+G) to see exactly where your screen center is.
    • Once you align perfectly for a game, press Alt+Shift+S to save it. Apply it anytime from the Display tab.
    • If a game uses Alt/Shift/Ctrl for its own controls, you may need to rebind Crosshair F's customizable hotkeys above.

    How to align the crosshair in your game

    1. Launch your game in Borderless Windowed mode. Fullscreen Exclusive mode will hide the overlay.
    2. Aim at a reference point in-game (a training dummy, wall dot, aim trainer target).
    3. Nudge the crosshair with Alt+Shift+Arrows (1 pixel) or Ctrl+Alt+Shift+Arrows (10 pixels) until it perfectly overlaps your game's aim point.
    4. Turn on debug grid with Alt+Shift+G to see exact screen center if needed.
    5. Save it with Alt+Shift+S and give it a name like "My Fortnite" or "CS2 Crouch". It appears below in "Your Custom Game Presets".
    6. Next time you play, click Apply on the saved preset. Done in 1 second.

    Every game renders its aim point slightly differently depending on your resolution, FOV, weapon, and stance. Manual calibration once per game gives you perfect accuracy forever. You can also browse community-shared presets in the Community tab » Game Positions.

    Help & About

    Fullscreen mode (read this)

    Crosshair F works perfectly in borderless windowed mode. Most modern games support this and it gives the same FPS as exclusive fullscreen with zero downside.

    If your game is in exclusive fullscreen, no overlay (Discord, Steam, CrosshairX, V2, this one, none) can render on top without injecting code into the game, which gets you banned. So we do not do that.

    Fix: open your game's video settings and switch from "Fullscreen" to "Borderless Windowed" or "Windowed Fullscreen". You will not lose any performance.

    Why Crosshair F is safe (no bans)

    • Does NOT inject into game processes
    • Does NOT read game memory
    • Does NOT hook keyboard or mouse input
    • Just a transparent always-on-top window, same as Discord overlay or your taskbar
    • Anti-cheats (VAC, EAC, BattlEye, Vanguard) do not flag it

    Note: even though the app itself is safe, some games (like Valorant with Vanguard) restrict ALL overlays as policy. Check your game's overlay policy.
